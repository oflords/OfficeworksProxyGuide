# Officeworks Proxy Guide
A simple way to print high quality proxies requiring very little upfront costs!

## Getting the PDF of cards
> So many sites offer decklist saving, most export options from other sites seem to work with MTGPrint regardless if you store lists elsewhere.
1. Make a decklist on [Moxfield](https://moxfield.com/) of cards you wish to print.
2. In the top left of the decklist, select More > Export
3. Click "Copy for Moxfield" to get the decklist in your clipboard
4. Head to [MTGPrint](https://mtgprint.net/), paste in the cards and click "Submit"
5. All the images for cards will appear below with a dropdown to select art styles. Tweak these to your liking
6. Before pressing print, tweak the settings for PDF generation. I would suggest the following:
   - Enable "Black Corners" if you want to avoid cutting round corners on your cards and they still look good
   - Disable all other toggles
   - Change Paper to "A4"
   - Set the Gap to "No Gap". This avoids small white borders on cards
7. Click the "Print" button and save the PDF

## Printing
> [!NOTE]
> We will be printing cards using an Australian office supplies chain called "Officeworks". The USA equivalent would be Staples/Office Depot. They should offer a similar service...

1. Head to [Officeworks Document Prints](https://www.officeworks.com.au/print-copy/p/document-prints-pcdhdpcp), select A4 for the Finished Size and click Create. You then select "Upload your design"
2. It will then prompt you for a file. Select the PDF you have made. Ensure it looks good and get to the "Print Options" step
3. Select "Colour", "Single Sided" and "Long Edge"
4. In the "Material Options" step, select White > 210gsm Gloss
> [!WARNING]
> These cards are about ~70% of the thickness of a real Magic card, so they will feel worse. Once sleeved its hard to notice.  
> If you want a better feel, select 300gsm Uncoated but know it will look worse. At time of writing they are the same price.
5. Continue forward, add it to card and checkout! Order ETAs usually claim multiple days until completion but orders usually fufill within 2 hours same day

## Cutting
The product you are looking for is an "A4 Cutter". Officeworks themselves has a large selection along with budget options from other stores. The more it costs, the better it will be and the cleaner edges will look. I personally used the "Studymate 22cm Paper Trimmer" which has dulled even when swapping blades after ~200 sheets and also is best chopping one sheet at a time.

## Other Tips
* If looking authentic is not something that bothers you, Moxfield has a "Get playtest cards" option next to the "Export" section. Cards from this option would probably look better in Black and White for cheaper options but are clearly proxies.
* Officeworks offers a walk in and print from emailed file option which is very convenient and cheap. It can print colour or black and white if you just wanted a quick card and did not have a printer.
